Henchman21.net. because CiscoDude.net was restricted.

I own very few domain names now, but that has not always been the case. When I first discovered hosting on the Internet I owned many domain names, short ones, long complex ones with dashes which were not condusive to phone exchanges, and some wishful project ones. After a while this got out of hand, silly, and expensive.

Having let all my domains lapse some years back, in May 2011, I decided I needed a domain name of my own to use. I decided on ciscodude.net, and registered it with Joker immediately. 7 months later, I wanted a valid SSL cert, so I purchased one from WHMCS for cn=secure.ciscodude.net. Several minutes later I had a valid cert. After reverse proxying many services through this certificate, configuration got too complex, and I set out to procure a wildcard cert.

I found a reseller who charged $94 US after tax for a Comodo PositiveSSL Wildcard, and generated a certificate request for cn=*.ciscodude.net. I was not immediately provided with a valid, signed wildcard cert. I started to get concerned and read the fine print, it seems that as my domain contained the word Cisco that they may have flagged it for additional "Brand Validation".

It seems at the sub-$100 level that validation is not done on the cert, but more to prevent certs which may be used to scam on other valid brands. I made it plenty clear that I this was to be used mostly on my network to prevent annoying SSL warnings, and not for commercial purposes, but Comodo denied the cert. I was refunded a credit, and I thought about my options. In a flash I registered henchman21.net and generated a new cert signing request for cn=*.henchman21.net and submitted it with my credit as payment. I was almost immediately presented with a valid, signed, wildcard cert.
